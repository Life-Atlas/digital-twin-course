The curriculum

4 Tracks. 12 Modules.

A structured journey from theory to implementation, across every major digital twin vertical.

Track A

Foundations

The theory, methodology, and standards that make digital twins interoperable

01

What Is a Digital Twin?

The Journey from Buildings to People

Distinguish digital twins from digital models and shadows. Understand the lifecycle perspective and maturity spectrum. See why the same principles that twin a building can twin a human body.

Case Study: WINNIIO Smart Heating — Self-learning twins on the edge in Swedish schools

Duration: 60-90 minutes

02

The SMILE Methodology

Impact First, Data Last

Master the six phases of SMILE (Sustainable Methodology for Impact Lifecycle Enablement). Apply the inverted pyramid: start with outcomes, work backward to data. Define a Minimal Viable Twin for any use case.

Exercise: SMILE Canvas workshop — apply Phase 1-2 to your own scenario

Downloadable: SMILE Canvas template

03

MIMs & Omni-Interoperability

Making Everything Talk to Everything

Master all 10 Minimal Interoperability Mechanisms (MIMs). Navigate legal, organizational, semantic, and technical interoperability layers. Design ontology-first architectures that are MIM-compliant from day one.

Exercise: MIM Maturity Assessment for your organization

Downloadable: MIM assessment tool + gap analysis template

Track B

Industries

Vertical deep dives showing how the same principles apply across radically different domains

04

Smart Buildings & Cities

Where Digital Twins Were Born

BIM/CIM/GIS integration. Local Digital Twins for municipalities. EU policy context. Self-learning heating systems.

Case Study: WINNIIO/VOFAB Smart Heating, Swedish Energy Agency

05

Manufacturing & Automotive

The Factory of the Future

ISO 23247 manufacturing twins. Product lifecycle twins. Supply chain visibility. Digital product passports.

Case Study: Heavy vehicle digital twins spanning manufacturing to fleet ops

06

The Biological Digital Twin

When the Subject Is Alive

Continuous-state models for living beings. Biological data complexity. Readiness scoring. What-if scenarios for biology.

Case Study: EquestRai at Mulawa Arabian Stud, Australia

07

Space & Planetary Twins

The View from Above

Earth observation data pipelines. GIS/BIM/CIM convergence at planetary scale. Digital Earth concept. Space-enabled precision.

Case Study: Satellite thermal imaging validating edge-sensor data

Track C

Life

The journey from building twins to biological twins to personal twins

08

The Personal Digital Twin

Your Life, Digitally Twinned

Why people are the most important thing to twin. Personal data sovereignty. WHO Quality of Life dimensions. Wearable integration. Proactive wellness.

Case Study: LifeAtlas — Building the personal digital twin platform

09

Precision Wellness & Longevity

From Reactive Healthcare to Proactive Life

WHO-grounded wellness intelligence. Health data regulations. Precision medicine. Elderly care at scale. Never-generic health nudges grounded in real data.

Case Study: Elderly care digital twin — behavioral analysis for 515K+ seniors

10

Edge-Native Intelligence

Intelligence Where You Are

Edge-first, cloud-fallback architectures. Data sovereignty at the personal level. Local-first patterns. Edge AI. Zero vendor lock-in.

Case Study: VOFAB edge deployment + LifeAtlas personal health processing

Track D

Implementation

How to actually build, deploy, and scale interoperable digital twins

11

The AI Journey

From Data to Explainable Decisions

Five stages of AI maturity. Fair AI (MIM5) in practice. RAG for hallucination-free AI. Agent architecture. Explainability anchored in reality.

Case Study: LifeAtlas AI Agent — 25 tools, capability scoping, prompt injection defense

12

Scaling, Ecosystem & Your DT Career

From Pilot to Production to Movement

Business case building. Ecosystem transaction management. EU/national funding. Career positioning. Open source strategy. The digital twin economy.

Case Study: Nicolas's cross-vertical journey — from Sweden to global

Track A (Foundations) and Track C (Life) are accessible to non-technical professionals. Tracks B and D go deeper into architecture and implementation. The workshop and strategy session include live guidance to help you navigate technical concepts regardless of your background.
SMILE starts with outcomes, not data. Most frameworks begin with "what data do we have?" and hope for value. SMILE inverts the pyramid: define the impact you want, then work backward to the minimal data needed. It is industry-agnostic, standards-aligned (MIMs, ISO 23247), and has been validated across buildings, manufacturing, equestrian, healthcare, and personal wellness.
